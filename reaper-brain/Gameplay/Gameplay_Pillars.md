# Gameplay Pillars

**Six Core Mechanical Systems**

---

## Pillar 1: Squad Command

**Core:** Direct your squad using tactical commands

### Commands Available
- **Stack Up** — Position squad at door/breach point
- **Hold Position** — Squad holds current location, maintains watch
- **Breach** — Execute coordinated door breach
- **Cover Sector** — Assign squad to cover zone of fire
- **Regroup** — Squad falls back to your position
- **Fire Support** — Suppressive fire on target area

### Mechanics
- Commands have execution time (0.5–3 seconds)
- Squad AI responds realistically (doesn't teleport)
- Cover selection is automatic (squad chooses best available)
- Communication is visual/radio (subtitled)
- Each squad member has skills (sniper, breacher, medic)

### Strategic Depth
- **Positioning matters:** Good cover = better accuracy, survival
- **Timing matters:** Commands must account for enemy positioning
- **Squad composition matters:** Different members suit different roles
- **Communication:** Clear orders = better execution

### Player Agency
- Can issue commands in real-time or pause to plan
- Can override squad AI (take direct control of individuals)
- Can assign fire zones or target priorities
- Can coordinate multi-squad movements

## Pillar 2: Stealth

**Core:** Avoid detection through sound, sight, and deception**

### Mechanics

#### Perception System
- **Sound Detection:**
  - Footsteps: Varies by surface (gravel=loud, carpet=quiet)
  - Weapons: Suppressed=quiet, unsuppressed=loud
  - Doors: Opening speed matters (fast=loud)
  - Equipment: Clanking gear (avoid running with loose items)

- **Sight Detection:**
  - Line of sight: Visual cone varies by enemy awareness level
  - Distance: Further = harder to detect
  - Lighting: Darkness masks, light exposes
  - Movement: Still targets are harder to see

- **Awareness Progression:**
  - **Unaware:** Routine patrol, no suspicion
  - **Suspicious:** Heard/saw something, investigating
  - **Searching:** Active hunt, heightened alert
  - **Alerted:** Full combat, calling for backup
  - **Aggressive:** All-in combat engagement

#### Tactical Stealth
- **Sync Takedowns:** Coordinated silent takedowns of multiple enemies
- **Suppressed Weapons:** Kill silently without alerting others
- **Noise Masking:** Use ambient noise to cover your movement
- **Last Known Position:** Enemy searches last position where you were seen
- **Detection Decay:** If you hide long enough, alert level decreases

#### Environmental Stealth
- **Lighting:** Stay in shadows, avoid spotlights
- **Cover:** Use objects to break sight lines
- **Height Advantage:** Roof access, elevated positions
- **Blind Spots:** Positions enemies can't see from patrols
- **Chokepoints:** Funnel enemies to controlled areas

### Gameplay Depth
- **Pure stealth runs:** Complete mission without firing a shot
- **Hybrid stealth:** Mix silent takedowns with careful combat
- **Stealth escape:** Get caught, then disappear
- **Noise management:** Plan routes to minimize sound

### Progression
- **Early missions:** Stealth is recommended
- **Mid-campaign:** Stealth is necessary (enemy numbers grow)
- **Late-game:** Stealth is tactical choice (combat is viable but costly)

## Pillar 3: Realistic Combat

**Core:** Gunfights are dangerous; every shot has weight**

### Mechanics

#### Weapon Realism
- **Recoil:** Automatic weapons have rising recoil (must compensate)
- **Sway:** Unsupported aim has drift (use cover for stability)
- **Suppression:** Heavy fire pins enemies down (limits accuracy)
- **Weapon Heat:** Sustained fire causes reliability issues
- **Ammunition:** Manage ammo, reload timing matters

#### Ballistic Reality
- **Cover Penetration:** Some cover can be shot through (light cover)
- **Bullet Spread:** Distance affects accuracy (aim carefully at range)
- **Ricochet:** Angles matter (indirect fire tactics)
- **Overpenetration:** Bullets pass through targets into allies (careful!)

#### Injury System
- **Health Damage:** Takes multiple hits to kill (enemy or player)
- **Injury Effects:** Wounded enemies move slower, aim worse
- **Bleed Out:** Mortally wounded enemies can be left to die or executed
- **Ally Wounds:** Squad members can be injured, affecting performance
- **Medical Supply:** Bandages, tourniquets, morphine (limited)

#### Combat Positioning
- **Cover Effectiveness:** Standing, crouching, prone offer different levels of safety
- **Exposure:** Blind corners = danger zones
- **Crossfire:** Enemies coordinate, create crossfires
- **Suppression Arcs:** Heavy fire limits enemy movement
- **Retreat Routes:** All positions have escape routes (for you and enemies)

### Tactical Depth
- **Weapon Selection:** Rifle, SMG, Pistol, Shotgun for different ranges
- **Loadout Customization:** Suppressors, scopes, extended mags
- **Ammunition Variety:** Standard, armor-penetrating, incendiary
- **Secondary Weapons:** Sidearms for backup
- **Equipment:** Grenades, smoke, flashbangs

### Difficulty Scaling
- **Easy:** Enemies are less accurate, health is higher, ammo is plentiful
- **Normal:** Baseline balance (designed experience)
- **Hard:** Enemies are deadly accurate, health is realistic, ammo is scarce
- **Hardcore:** Permadeath (single life, restart on death)

## Pillar 4: Enemy AI Awareness

**Core:** Enemies adapt to your tactics; no two encounters are identical**

### Awareness States

| State | Behavior | Detection | Lethality |
|-------|----------|-----------|-----------|
| **Unaware** | Routine patrol, idle chatter | High threshold | Low threat |
| **Suspicious** | Investigate sounds/movement | Medium threshold | Medium threat |
| **Searching** | Active hunt for threats | Low threshold | High threat |
| **Alerted** | Combat ready, calling for backup | Automatic | Very high threat |
| **Aggressive** | Full engagement, coordinated tactics | Aware of all | Maximum threat |

### Tactical Behavior
- **Patrol Routes:** Enemies follow patterns but vary slightly (not robotic)
- **Cover Selection:** Enemies automatically seek best available cover
- **Flanking:** Groups attempt to maneuver around your position
- **Suppressive Fire:** Heavy fire pins you down (tactical, not random)
- **Retreat:** Enemies pull back when outnumbered (not suicidal)
- **Communication:** Radio calls, hand signals, verbal coordination
- **Reinforcements:** Nearby units respond to alerts (10–60 seconds)

### Adaptation
- **Learning:** If stealth works, enemies don't fall for same trick twice
- **Coordination:** Multiple groups coordinate tactics against you
- **Improvisation:** When plans fail, enemies adapt on the fly
- **Morale:** Taking heavy casualties affects enemy aggression
- **Leadership:** Killing leaders (officers) impacts squad coordination

### Asymmetrical Threat
- **Sniper Positions:** Some enemies have elevated vantage points
- **Heavy Weapons:** Mounted MGs, grenade launchers available
- **Vehicles:** Armored transport, heavy weapons support
- **Numbers:** Enemy forces scale with mission difficulty
- **Equipment:** Body armor affects damage required

## Pillar 5: Civilian Presence

**Core:** Your actions affect innocent lives; every civilian matters**

### Civilian AI
- **Non-combatant:** Civilians don't fight back; they flee or hide
- **Panic Response:** Loud noises trigger panic; panic exposes your position
- **Shelter Seeking:** Civilians move toward safe areas (buildings, bunkers)
- **Communication:** Civilians call for help (police, military)
- **Collateral Damage:** Civilian casualties are tracked and weighted morally

### Civilian Interaction
- **Dialogue:** Can communicate with civilians (translations provided)
- **Extraction:** Can move civilians to safe zones
- **Sacrifice:** Can use civilians as cover (morally costly)
- **Avoidance:** Can bypass civilians entirely
- **Protection:** Can position squad to guard civilians

### Moral Tracking
- **Civilian Casualties:** Every death is recorded
- **Rescue Rate:** How many civilians did you save?
- **Collateral Damage:** Unintended civilian deaths carry weight
- **Morale Impact:** Squad morale drops if civilians are harmed
- **Ending Impact:** Civilian casualties affect which endings are available

### Gameplay Mechanics
- **Detection:** Panicked civilians attract enemy attention
- **Slowdown:** Escorting civilians slows your movement
- **Vulnerability:** Civilians can be targets (enemies use them)
- **Rescue Points:** Safe zones where civilians must be delivered
- **Time Pressure:** Civilians have limited survival time in danger zones

## Pillar 6: Moral Choices

**Core:** Your decisions shape Elias and determine which ending is possible**

### Choice Categories

#### Tactical Choices
- **Stealth vs Combat:** Which approach for this situation?
- **Speed vs Caution:** Rush or take time?
- **Aggressive vs Defensive:** Overwhelm or contain?
- **Individual vs Team:** Sacrifice one to save many?

#### Moral Choices
- **Civilian Safety:** How much risk to protect innocents?
- **Prisoner Treatment:** Execute, capture, or release?
- **Squad Sacrifice:** Will you abandon a soldier?
- **Objective vs Humanity:** Complete mission or save lives?

#### Leadership Choices
- **Support Allies:** Back [[Daniel Ruiz|Ruiz]] or [[Marcus Kane|Kane]]?
- **Enforce Orders:** Strict discipline or flexibility?
- **Conflict Resolution:** Mediate or let it fester?
- **Final Authority:** Who decides what happens?

### Consequence Tracking
- **Karma System:** Choices accumulate morally
- **Relationship Tracking:** Allies notice your patterns
- **Dialogue Changes:** Squad reacts to your choices
- **Ending Lock-In:** Too many aggressive choices lock out pacifist endings
- **Narrative Branches:** Major choices create story splits

### No Perfect Solutions
- **Every choice has cost:** Nothing is free
- **Morality is gray:** Right and wrong aren't clear
- **Consequences appear later:** Choices matter in ways you don't expect
- **Player identity:** Your choices define Elias for you
