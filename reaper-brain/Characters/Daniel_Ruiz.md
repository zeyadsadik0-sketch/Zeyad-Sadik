## Character Archetype

**The Idealist**
- Young, still believes in clear good vs evil
- Sees the best in people, especially [[Elias "Reaper" Haddad|Elias]]
- Reminds Elias of who he used to be
- Vulnerable; his fate tests Elias's protective instincts
- Represents hope in a hopeless war

---

## Core Traits

### Strengths
- **Loyalty:** Trusts Elias completely; would follow him anywhere
- **Optimism:** Believes they can do this the right way
- **Courage:** Young enough to be fearless; old enough to understand fear
- **Idealism:** Questions orders when they seem wrong
- **Humanity:** Never lets the war harden him (at least, not yet)

### Weaknesses
- **Inexperience:** Makes tactical mistakes, misses subtle dangers
- **Idealism:** Can be a liability in a morally gray conflict
- **Emotional:** Shows his feelings openly, unlike [[Elias "Reaper" Haddad|Elias]]
- **Dependence:** Relies on Elias for validation and guidance
- **Vulnerability:** Fragile moral center; could break if he sees too much

---

## Background

Ruiz enlisted straight out of high school, driven by patriotism and a need to prove himself. This is his first deployment. He joined believing in the mission: stop the insurgency, protect civilians, make a difference.

He hasn't yet experienced the moral compromises that broke Elias at [[Checkpoint Twelve]].

The game is his crucible — will he become another [[Marcus Kane|Kane]], or will he survive with his idealism intact?

---

## Relationship with [[Elias "Reaper" Haddad]]

### The Mentor-Mentee Dynamic
- **Ruiz's View:** "Elias knows the right way. I trust him completely."
- **Elias's View:** "I have to keep this kid from becoming like me. Haunted."
- **The Connection:** Father-figure relationship; Elias sees his younger self

### Emotional Beats
- **Act I:** Ruiz hero-worships Elias; Elias is protective but distant
- **Act II:** They bond through quiet moments; Elias opens up slightly
- **Act III:** Ruiz's idealism is tested; Elias must choose between saving the mission or saving Ruiz

### The Stakes
- If Ruiz dies, Elias loses hope that anything good can survive
- If Ruiz is corrupted, Elias failed at the one thing he can control
- If Ruiz survives uncorrupted, it proves Elias's way works

---

## Conflict with [[Marcus Kane]]

Ruiz and Kane represent two paths:
- **Kane:** "This is war. It's brutal. Accept it."
- **Ruiz:** "This is war. We can still be good. Right?"

Elias is caught between them, trying to mentor Ruiz away from Kane's path.

## Voice & Dialogue

**Tone:** Eager, earnest, occasionally nervous. Respectful authority, supportive of teammates.

**Speech Patterns:**
- Calls people by rank respectfully
- Asks questions when unsure (shows humility)
- Uses humor to manage fear (nervous energy)
- Direct but gentle

**Example Dialogue:**
> "Sergeant, you said we're the good guys. That hasn't changed, has it?"

> "I don't understand the order, but I trust you. Just... can you explain it to me?"

## Character Arc Through Missions

### [[Dust Line]] & [[Concrete Maze]]
- Green, excited, eager to prove himself
- Makes minor tactical errors; learns from them

### [[Silent Watch]] & [[Blackout|Operation Blackout]]
- Begins to understand the moral complexity
- Questions Elias for the first time; Elias respects the question

### [[Burning Streets]]
- Faces a moment where being "good" costs the mission
- Breaks emotionally; questions everything

### [[Last Signal]] — The Test
- Ruiz's fate in the final mission decides whether hope survives
- Elias must decide if saving Ruiz is worth the cost to the mission
- This choice defines Elias's character arc

## Narrative Purpose

Ruiz embodies the question: **Can innocence survive in this war?**

He's the part of Elias that hasn't broken yet — the Elias from before Checkpoint Twelve.

If Ruiz dies, Elias loses hope. If Ruiz survives uncorrupted, Elias has proof that his way works.
