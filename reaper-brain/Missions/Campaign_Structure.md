# Campaign Structure

**Six Missions Across Three Acts**

---

## Campaign at a Glance

| Act | Missions | Theme | Elias's State |
|-----|----------|-------|--------------|
| **I: ARRIVAL** | 1–2 | Arrival, learning the AO | Confident, cautious |
| **II: SHADOW OPS** | 3–4 | Deeper, personal stakes | Protective, conflicted |
| **III: COLLAPSE** | 5–6 | Everything falls apart | Desperate, questioning |

---

## ACT I: ARRIVAL

### Mission 1: [[Dust Line]]

**Objectives:**
- Establish forward operating position
- Observe primary target area
- Engage only if necessary

**Gameplay Focus:**
- Introduce squad command system
- First encounter with [[Marcus Kane|Kane]'s aggression
- First civilian encounter

**Character Moments:**
- Elias and [[Daniel Ruiz|Ruiz]] first meet
- Kane pushes for faster, more aggressive approach
- Elias overrules Kane; demonstrates leadership style

**Key Decision:**
- Civilian family crosses AO: extract, bypass, or ignore?

**Outcome:**
- Successfully establish position
- First friction with Kane
- Ruiz impressed by Elias's restraint

---

### Mission 2: [[Concrete Maze]]

**Objectives:**
- Patrol urban area
- Locate secondary target
- Avoid civilian casualties

**Gameplay Focus:**
- Urban infiltration, stealth mechanics
- Squad positioning and cover
- Civilian AI behavior

**Character Moments:**
- [[Marcus Kane|Kane]] grows more vocal about Elias's caution
- [[Daniel Ruiz|Ruiz]] questions an order; Elias respects it
- First real combat encounter

**Key Decision:**
- Combat in civilian area: adapt tactics or minimize exposure by bypassing?

**Outcome:**
- Target located, but area is compromised
- [[Marcus Kane|Kane]] and Elias's conflict becomes obvious
- [[Daniel Ruiz|Ruiz]] begins to develop his own judgment

---

## ACT II: SHADOW OPERATIONS

### Mission 3: [[Silent Watch]]

**Objectives:**
- Surveillance of high-value target
- Gather intelligence
- No engagement unless direct threat

**Gameplay Focus:**
- Extended stealth observation
- Enemy behavior patterns
- Moral ambiguity

**Character Moments:**
- Elias confides in [[Daniel Ruiz|Ruiz]]; begins mentorship
- [[Marcus Kane|Kane]] openly challenges Elias's orders
- Observation reveals complex civilian/insurgent overlap

**Key Decision:**
- Target has family nearby: continue surveillance, warn them, abort mission?

**Outcome:**
- Intelligence gathered with minimal civilian impact
- [[Marcus Kane|Kane]] and Elias's relationship approaches breaking point
- [[Daniel Ruiz|Ruiz]] becomes Elias's anchor

---

### Mission 4: [[Blackout|Operation Blackout]]

**Signature Mission. Stealth hostage rescue. 2:13 AM.**

**Objectives:**
- Infiltrate compound (2:13 AM)
- Rescue hostage alive
- Extract without civilian casualties

**Gameplay Focus:**
- Perfect stealth sequence
- **Critical Choice:** Breach silently or explosively?
  - **Silent breach:** Maintains stealth, prevents alarm, but slower
  - **Explosive breach:** Breaks cover, triggers combat, but gains speed
- Both lead to same climax (alarm, extraction pressure)

**Character Moments:**
- Elias finally leads a mission entirely his way
- [[Daniel Ruiz|Ruiz]] follows without question
- [[Marcus Kane|Kane]] is marginalized (his aggression not needed)

**Key Decision:**
- Breach method (reflects Elias's philosophy)
- Hostage rescue approach (reflects moral commitment)

**Outcome:**
- Success (either way, but with different consequences)
- Elias's caution is validated
- For a moment, Elias believes his way works
- [[Marcus Kane|Kane]] is proven wrong (he thinks)

---

## ACT III: COLLAPSE

### Mission 5: [[Burning Streets]]

**Objectives:**
- Establish presence in urban combat zone
- Protect civilians being evacuated
- Engage enemy forces

**Gameplay Focus:**
- Urban chaos, multiple objectives, civilian AI
- Impossible choices (save civilians vs complete objective)
- [[Marcus Kane|Kane]] reasserts dominance (his aggression is now needed)

**Character Moments:**
- Everything Elias planned is falling apart
- [[Daniel Ruiz|Ruiz]] sees his first major civilian casualty
- [[Marcus Kane|Kane]]'s "I told you so" attitude; Elias questions himself
- [[Daniel Ruiz|Ruiz]] breaks emotionally

**Key Decision:**
- Prioritize objectives or civilian lives? Can't do both.

**Outcome:**
- Mission partially successful
- Civilian casualties occur despite Elias's caution
- [[Daniel Ruiz|Ruiz]] is shaken; questions Elias's leadership
- [[Marcus Kane|Kane]] regains credibility

---

### Mission 6: [[Last Signal]]

**Final Mission. The test of everything.**

**Objectives:**
- Achieve primary objective (varies by context)
- Survive
- Protect squad (especially [[Daniel Ruiz|Ruiz]])

**Gameplay Focus:**
- All systems combined: stealth, combat, civilian protection, squad command
- Multiple valid solutions (different ways to "win")
- Moral weight: every choice has a cost

**Character Moments:**
- Elias must articulate his answer to the core question
- [[Daniel Ruiz|Ruiz]]'s fate is decided by Elias's choices
- [[Marcus Kane|Kane]] either proves his way or dies proving Elias's way
- Final confrontation of philosophies

**Key Decision(s):**
- Save the objective or save your team?
- Believe in Elias's way or Kane's way?
- What matters most: mission, people, ideals, or survival?

**Possible Outcomes:**
1. **Professional success, personal cost:** Mission complete, [[Daniel Ruiz|Ruiz]] or [[Marcus Kane|Kane]] dies
2. **Personal victory, mission failure:** [[Daniel Ruiz|Ruiz]] saved, objective compromised
3. **Both succeed:** Requires perfect execution (possible but difficult)
4. **Both fail:** The harsh reality option
5. **Pyrrhic victory:** Mission complete, squad broken, moral cost unbearable

## Thematic Through-Lines

### [[Checkpoint Twelve]] Echoes
Each mission asks: "How do you prevent another Checkpoint Twelve?"

### [[Marcus Kane]] Conflict
Progressive escalation: friction → open conflict → potential mutiny → reckoning

### [[Daniel Ruiz]] Arc
Idealism → first trauma → emotional break → final test

### Elias's Belief System
Certainty → doubt → action → crisis → redefinition
